01 / THE CONFLICT

The Problem

Aircraft and missiles send telemetry — altitude, speed, position, sensor data — back to the ground as radio signals. The frequency bands they use now overlap with 5G and LTE networks. When a cell tower transmits over the same band, the interference can be stronger than the telemetry signal itself, causing the signal to be completely lost.

02 / LIMITS OF DSP

Why Traditional Receivers Fail

Classical receivers were built to pull a signal out of random background static. They assume interference is weak and noise-like. But 5G/LTE isn't random noise — it's a strong, structured signal sitting right on top of yours. A traditional receiver can't tell the two apart, so it rejects or distorts both, failing to extract any telemetry.

03 / CONFORMER EXCISION

What This Does

This is a deep-learning receiver that learns what cellular interference looks like and separates it from the telemetry signal — like picking out one voice in a loud room instead of just turning the volume down. A neural network reconstructs the clean signal, and error-correction coding fixes whatever remains. The result: the original message is recovered exactly, even when the interference outpowers the signal.

NOTE Results shown are on a simulated RF channel.

Core Excision Engineering

🎯

Segmented Sync

Classic correlation is split into 4 segments of 8 µs. Combines GPS-style acquisition grids to withstand severe carrier frequency offsets up to ±240 kHz.

⚡

Spectral Excision

SpectralGate maps incoming noisy IQ spectra into standardized log-magnitude/phase domains to selectively isolate and excise wideband blockers.

🧠

Conformer Blocks

Conformer layers utilize both self-attention and depth-wise convolutions to capture long-range temporal dependencies and restore phase integrity.

🛡️

Min-Sum LDPC

Calibrated Log-Likelihood Ratios feed a rate-1/2 LDPC decoder (50 max iterations), restoring corrupted signals error-free even during severe jammer overlaps.

How Errors Get Corrected

Error-correction coding (LDPC) rescues bits the model gets wrong
Data Block (1025 bits) Parity Block (1021 bits)
📡

1. Encode (Codeword)

Your message (1025 bits) is expanded with parity bits (1021 bits) to create a 2046-bit codeword.

2. Corrupted Channel

RF noise and LTE interference flip ~7% of the codeword bits to red errors (about 77 bits in total).

3. Corrected (FEC)

The LDPC decoder identifies and flips the error bits back to green, recovering the original data perfectly.

“This is why the demo can show a garbled raw output and a perfect final message at the same time — the model does the heavy lifting, and the code cleans up the rest.”

Rate-1/2 means half the transmitted bits are redundancy. More redundancy = more errors correctable, at the cost of throughput. This project uses IRIG-106-class LDPC codes.

Limitations & Open Challenges

This is a validated research prototype — not a fielded system. Here's an honest account of what it does not yet do.

⏱️

Throughput & Latency

The receiver currently runs at approximately 0.75× real-time on an NVIDIA L4 GPU — it processes data slightly slower than it arrives. A deployed range receiver needs to sustain ~3,900 frames/second continuously.

Paths to close the gap: Better deployment hardware (A100/H100 + TensorRT), a hybrid classical/neural receiver, or a distilled smaller model.

📡

Hardware End-to-End Testing

All results are measured on a simulated RF channel. The system has not yet been tested on signals captured from real radio hardware (SDR, actual antenna, real 5G traffic).

Next phase: Sim-to-real evaluation on replayed real cellular recordings, then live SDR captures with a held-out real test set.

📊

Narrowband Tone Jamming

Continuous narrowband tones at equal power produce a higher raw BER (~2.5%). Error-correction still recovers the message, but the safety margin is thinner and could be exceeded under stronger jamming.

Planned fix: A larger multi-frame model that exploits temporal context across neighboring frames.

Technical Architecture & Signal Chain

Detailed trace of the telemetry receiver chain. The Conformer-based pipeline replaces traditional FIR filters and threshold decoders.

Step-by-Step Model Architecture Pipeline

3D Neural Receiver Architecture Model

Visual model design: SpectralGate applies learned soft-frequency excision to remove 5G/LTE blockers, the convolutional U-Net Denoiser performs time-domain waveform recovery, and the Conformer Stack decodes temporal bit sequences.

End-to-End Signal Chain Detail

📡 Part A: Forward Chain (Transmission & Impairments)

The channel simulator generates a physically accurate telemetry signal and subjects it to real-world RF link impairments:

  1. Digital Bits to NRZ: 256 bits map to bipolar NRZ symbols (0 → -1, 1 → +1) at 10 samples per bit (1 Mbps over 10 MHz), producing a 2560-sample baseband stream.
  2. Gaussian Pulse Shaping: The NRZ signal is shaped via a Gaussian filter (BT=0.5, σ≈2.65 samples) to smooth transitions and restrict the spectrum.
  3. FM Modulation (Digital → IQ): The shaped baseband signal shifts carrier frequency by ±350 kHz. Phase integration yields exp(j·φ(t)), a constant-envelope complex IQ waveform.
  4. Oscillator & Motion Drift: Random-walk Wiener phase noise (5-200 Hz linewidth) and Doppler shift (±25 kHz) model hardware drift and vehicle velocity.
  5. Cellular Co-Channel interference: CP-OFDM LTE and 5G NR (256-QAM) blockers are generated and summed directly onto the telemetry band.
  6. LNA Saturation: Under high interference, the receiver's Low-Noise Amplifier saturates, compressing amplitudes and generating third-order IM3 intermod products.
  7. Gain Scaling & Noise: An Automatic Gain Control loop normalizes power to -10 dBm. Finally, AWGN is added for the target SNR.

🧠 Part B: Reverse Chain (Excision & Demodulation)

The receiver performs synchronization, extracts the signal, excises interference, and decodes the message:

  1. Frame Synchronization: A segmented correlation bank searches over Time-Lag × CFO to lock on the 320-sample sync word pattern (Lock threshold PSL ≥ 1.5).
  2. CFO Derotation & Scaling: Sliced 2560-sample frames are derotated to remove CFO, and gain is scaled back to -10 dBm to satisfy the model's amplitude constraints.
  3. Spectral Excision (SpectralGate): Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) vectors enter the model, where the SpectralGate applies dynamic frequency-domain masking.
  4. CNN & Conformer Layers: The U-Net denoiser extracts clean phase features, and 4 Conformer blocks model long-range temporal bit dependencies, outputting 256 bit logits.
  5. LLR Calibration: Raw focal-loss logits are temperature-calibrated (T ≈ 0.55) to output honest Log-Likelihood Ratios (LLRs).
  6. LDPC Belief Propagation: Codewords spanning 10 frames are checked iteratively (Min-Sum decoder, 50 max iterations) to locate and correct remaining bit flips.
  7. Descrambling to ASCII: Corrected bits are descrambled via an LFSR keystream XOR, grouped into bytes, and converted to ASCII characters to yield the final text.

Detailed Technical Glossary

A comprehensive dictionary of terms, layers, and metrics in the neural telemetry link.

RF (Radio Frequency)
High-frequency electromagnetic waves used to transmit flight data wirelessly.
IQ (In-Phase / Quadrature)
Complex representation of a signal split into real (I) and imaginary (Q) components.
PCM-FM (Pulse Code Modulation - FM)
Telemetry standard (IRIG-106) where digital bits modulate carrier frequency offsets.
FSW (Frame Synchronization Word)
A unique 32-bit marker (0xFE6B2840) prepended to frames to lock alignment.
CFO (Carrier Frequency Offset)
Frequency misalignment between local and remote oscillators due to drift or Doppler.
SJR (Signal-to-Jammer Ratio)
Power ratio between the telemetry signal and co-channel cellular interference (dB).
SNR (Signal-to-Noise Ratio)
Power ratio between the telemetry signal and static background thermal noise (dB).
AWGN (Additive White Gaussian Noise)
Mathematical noise model representing random thermal static in electrical channels.
LNA (Low-Noise Amplifier)
First-stage receiver amplifier; operates in compression (acting as a limiter) under jamming.
AGC (Automatic Gain Control)
Feedback loop that normalizes the received signal power before feeding it to the model.
Spectral Excision
Frequency-domain filtering that selectively zeroes out narrow/wideband interference peaks.
SpectralGate Layer
FFT-based learned dynamic frequency mask to excise cellular blockers.
U-Net Denoiser
An encoder-decoder network with skip connections to reconstruct clean baseband waveforms.
Conformer Stack
Four attention+convolution layers that perform temporal modeling and decode bit sequences.
Logit
Raw, unscaled real-valued outputs from the neural network representing bit confidence.
LLR (Log-Likelihood Ratio)
Temperature-calibrated soft probability values used directly by the LDPC decoder.
FEC (Forward Error Correction)
Redundant data encoding that allows the receiver to detect and correct bit errors.
LDPC (Low-Density Parity-Check)
High-efficiency linear error-correcting block code used to fix residual bit errors.
Min-Sum Algorithm
An iterative parity-check verification algorithm used to decode LDPC codewords.
BER (Bit Error Rate)
The percentage of erroneous bits out of the total received bits.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the model handle out-of-distribution (OOD) interference? ▾
The excision engine utilizes domain randomization during training (v17 generator). It trains on dynamic wideband OFDM, multi-tone jammers, Swept chirps, and pulsed noise. This ensures the Conformer generalizes across standard LTE and 5G interference layers without crashing.
Why is LLR calibration needed before LDPC decoding? ▾
Conformer output logits are computed using focal-loss gradients, which typical mis-calibrates probability outputs. By fitting a temperature scaling parameter T on the validation set, we restore honest, calibrated Log-Likelihood Ratios necessary for optimal min-sum LDPC checks.
Does this run locally in the browser or on the server? ▾
The signal processing and conformer inference are executed server-side (Hugging Face Spaces) in Python for numerical accuracy. Downsampled wave structures are then pushed via JSON to the browser client, which plots them using lightweight HTML5 canvas engines.

More FAQs

Expanded questions and answers on neural receiver design, channel constraints, and implementation details.

What is this project? ▾
A deep-learning radio receiver that recovers telemetry data — altitude, speed, position, sensor readings — from signals corrupted by 5G/LTE interference, even when the interference is stronger than the signal itself.
Why can't normal receivers do this? ▾
Classical receivers were designed to pull a signal out of weak, random background noise. 5G/LTE is the opposite: a strong, structured signal that overlaps yours in the same frequency band. Traditional methods can't separate two signals sharing a band — so they fail.
What does "the interference is stronger than the signal" mean? ▾
It's measured as signal-to-jammer ratio (SJR). At 0 dB the interference has equal power to the signal; at −5 dB it's about three times stronger. Classical receivers break down well before that point — this system keeps working.
How accurate is it? ▾
The neural network alone recovers about 93% of frames perfectly. After error-correction coding, the message comes through with zero frame errors across the tested range — including when interference equals or exceeds signal power.
Is this tested on real radio hardware? ▾
Not yet. All results are on a physically-modeled, simulated RF channel. Real-hardware testing with software-defined radios is the next phase of the project. (We state this plainly — simulation results are a starting point, not a field-deployment claim.)
What's the hardest case it faces? ▾
Continuous narrowband jamming sitting directly on the carrier at equal power (like a continuous-wave jammer or strong spur). There the model leaves more errors, but error-correction still recovers the message — with a smaller safety margin than in other cases.
Does it run in real time? ▾
Currently about 0.75× real-time on a mid-tier GPU — slightly slower than data arrives. There are three clear paths to close the gap: better deployment hardware with proper optimization, a hybrid design that only engages the neural network when interference is detected, or a smaller distilled model.
What makes it novel? ▾
There is no published method that recovers telemetry bits when co-channel cellular interference is stronger than the signal. This system does — and it's validated end to end (synchronization, the model, and error correction working together), not just as a model in isolation.
How does the demo work? ▾
You type a message; it's encoded into a radio waveform, corrupted by simulated interference and noise, then recovered by the receiver. You watch the signal at every stage — clean, interfered, received, recovered — and see the message come back error-free after error correction.
What is PCM-FM telemetry? ▾
PCM-FM is the standard modulation (per the IRIG-106 telemetry standard) used to send digital data from aircraft, missiles, and test vehicles back to ground stations. It encodes bits as small shifts in a radio carrier's frequency.
Why deep learning instead of a classical filter? ▾
A fixed filter can only remove interference whose shape you know in advance. A learned model adapts to the structure of real cellular interference — which varies with traffic, scheduling, and bandwidth — and separates it from the telemetry signal, something a static filter cannot do.

Why This Matters: Significance & Motivation

Resilience under interference is a critical requirement in modern defense-sponsored and civilian communications.

📡

Congested & Contested Spectrum

Defense and flight-test telemetry increasingly shares frequency bands with commercial 5G and LTE. Reliable data recovery under co-channel interference has become a baseline requirement, not a rare edge case.

🛡️

Resilience in Degraded RF & EW

The ability to recover a signal when something stronger is sitting on top of it is foundational to operating in crowded or contested spectrum — a central concern of modern, electronic-warfare-aware system design.

⚠️

Mission & Safety Critical

Flight-test and range telemetry carries safety-of-flight and mission-critical information. Losing frames to interference is not an inconvenience — it is a critical data-integrity and aerospace safety problem.

🧠

AI-Defined Radio Systems

Learned receivers that adapt to the structure of interference — rather than relying on fixed classical filters — represent a generational shift in how resilient communication and signal-intelligence systems are built.

🛫

Dual-Use Aerospace Value

Solving telemetry-versus-cellular coexistence has direct civilian benefit (aviation, congested-band IoT and urban communications) alongside its defense relevance — the same capability serves both.

🇮🇳

National Spectrum Coexistence

As more services compete for the same bands, the capacity to extract a wanted signal from a crowded, overlapping RF environment is increasingly strategic — for safety, for communications reliability, and for situational awareness.
